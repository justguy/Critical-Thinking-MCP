## Response

```text
You can’t guarantee “nobody feels excluded again” in a month, but you can make exclusion much less likely and improve morale fast with a very explicit cadence:

Week 1:
- Tell the team your standard: respectful inclusion, equal airtime, no cliques, no surprises.
- Run a private 20-minute 1:1 with each person. Ask: “When do you feel left out here?”, “What helps you do your best work?”, “What should change this month?”
- Fix obvious meeting issues immediately: no interruptions, round-robin input, written agendas, notes shared with everyone.

Week 2:
- Create team norms in writing: response expectations, meeting behavior, decision-making, how credit is given, and how concerns are raised.
- Check work distribution for exclusion patterns: who gets visibility, stretch work, context, and praise. Rebalance if needed.
- Start recognizing contributions publicly every week, spread across the whole team.

Week 3:
- Hold a team discussion on collaboration: what helps people feel included, what shuts people down, and what the team wants to keep/change.
- Pair people across subgroups on small tasks so relationships are not siloed.
- Address any repeat exclusion behavior privately and directly the same day.

Week 4:
- Re-run short 1:1 check-ins: “What improved?”, “What still feels off?”, “Do you feel more included than 3 weeks ago?”
- Share 3 concrete changes made from feedback so people see action, not just listening.
- Keep the practices that worked and set one measurable goal for next month.

Non-negotiables for you:
- No favorites.
- No private context that others need to do their jobs.
- No public criticism.
- Credit in public, correction in private.
- Follow up fast when someone says they feel excluded.
```
